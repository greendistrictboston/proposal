The Concept
The Green District is a proposed carbon nuetral, 100% affordable new city district in Boston, MA. Situated on 22 acres of land reclaimed from the Bass River (an inlet of Boston Harbor), the Green District connects The Financial District and the South Boston Waterfront. Comprised of 33 12 story buildings, internconnected by a wooden boulevard and two greenways. Each 12 story building will be anchored by a ground floor independent retailer, with 11 stories of 100% income restricted rental units above. The District has 5,082 residential units in all, providing housing for approximately 10,000 people. Additionally, 100% of the District's electricity consumption will be fufilled by the photovoltaic solar panels located on all 33 building's roofs, and a central geothermal well will provide heating and cooling, enabling full carbon neutrality. The Green District is a transit oriented development, located less than 400ft (1 block) from South Station Train and Bus Station, enabling all residents of the Green District to seamlessly commute via environmentally friendly mass transit systems. All told, The Green District will bring 10,000 Bostonians access to dynamically located, income restricted affordable housing that is fully carbon neutral.
